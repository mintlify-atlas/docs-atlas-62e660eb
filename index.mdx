QuickLook Video is a free, open-source macOS app that fills the gap between what macOS natively understands and the wide world of video formats. Install it once, and Finder automatically shows thumbnails and cover art, QuickLook shows full previews, and Spotlight indexes metadata — all for file types that macOS would otherwise display as blank icons.

## What QuickLook Video adds to macOS

macOS natively supports a limited set of media formats — primarily MPEG audio and video inside MPEG containers. QuickLook Video extends this with:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Finder thumbnails" icon="image">
    Static snapshot thumbnails and embedded cover art for non-native video and audio files, displayed directly in Finder.
  </Card>
  <Card title="QuickLook previews" icon="eye">
    Press Space on any supported file in Finder to see a full preview via the QuickLook extension.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Spotlight metadata" icon="magnifying-glass">

  <Card title="Wide codec support" icon="microchip">

## Get started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download the app">
    Get the latest release from the [GitHub releases page](https://github.com/Marginal/QuickLookVideo/releases). Open the `.dmg` and drag **QuickLook Video.app** to your Applications folder.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch the app">
    Open **QuickLook Video** from your Applications folder. The app registers all the necessary system extensions on first launch.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Browse your video library">
    Open a Finder window containing MKV, WebM, AVI, or other non-native video files. Thumbnails should appear automatically. Press **Space** on any file to see a QuickLook preview.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Search with Spotlight">
    Use Spotlight (⌘Space) to search video files by title, codec, resolution, or other metadata. Indexing runs in the background and may take a few minutes on first run.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
QuickLook Video requires macOS 12 (Monterey) or later and a Mac with AVX2 support (late-2013 or newer).
</Note>
